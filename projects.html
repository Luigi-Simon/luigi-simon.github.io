2025 · Hackathon

PlaquePal

An AI-augmented handheld ultrasound system designed for cardiovascular screening. The goal: make plaque detection accessible outside of traditional clinical settings.

We used MediaPipe computer vision for real-time telemetry, allowing the device to track and analyze imagery on the fly rather than requiring a specialist to interpret results after the fact.

What I learned

  • Real-time computer vision pipelines with MediaPipe
  • Designing for hardware-software integration under hackathon time pressure
  • How to communicate technical health-tech concepts to non-technical judges

2025 · Side project

Study Royale

A productivity-focused game that turns studying into a play experience. The design borrows core loops from popular mobile games — progression, rewards, light competition — and applies them to focused study sessions.

The project was equal parts design exercise and code experiment: how do you make something genuinely motivating without making it manipulative?

What I explored

  • Game design principles applied to non-game contexts
  • Prototyping interactive UI quickly
  • The line between motivation and dark patterns
